The old lighthouse stood on the rocky cliff above the stormy sea. Every night the lighthouse keeper climbed the narrow stairs to light the lamp. The lamp burned bright and warned passing ships away from the dangerous rocks below the cliff.

The keeper's name was Edgar, and Edgar had tended the lighthouse for thirty years. Edgar knew every wave and every storm that struck the cliff. The sea was Edgar's only companion, and the lighthouse was Edgar's only home.

One stormy night, a young sailor named Marlow lost his ship near the rocks. The storm tossed Marlow's small boat against the dangerous waves. Marlow saw the lighthouse lamp shining through the storm and rowed his boat toward the light.

Edgar saw the small boat struggling in the stormy sea. Edgar lit an extra lamp and called out to Marlow through the wind. The sailor steered his boat carefully around the rocks, guided by the lighthouse lamp that Edgar kept burning.

Marlow finally reached the rocky shore below the cliff. Edgar climbed down the narrow stairs to help the exhausted sailor. The storm slowly passed, and the sea grew calm again under the lighthouse lamp.

Marlow stayed with Edgar in the lighthouse for many days. Edgar taught Marlow about the sea, the storms, and the dangerous rocks near the cliff. Marlow learned to climb the narrow stairs and tend the lamp through the long stormy nights.

Years later, Marlow became a lighthouse keeper himself, tending his own lamp on a different rocky cliff above a different stormy sea. Marlow never forgot Edgar, the old keeper who saved him from the storm, or the lighthouse lamp that guided his small boat through the dangerous waves to safety.
